Fire Hydrant Systems

Intro

A fire hydrant system is a network of pipes and valves that are designed to provide an adequate supply of water to firefighters in the event of a fire. The system typically includes a series of underground pipes connected to a network of fire hydrants. These hydrants are typically located at strategic points around a building or property, making it easy for firefighters to connect their hoses and begin extinguishing the fire.

How does it work?

Several types of fire hydrant systems exist, including wet systems, dry systems, and pre-action systems. Each type of system is designed for different types of buildings and fire hazards. Wet systems are the most common and are used in buildings where the temperature is above freezing. Dry systems are used in buildings where the temperature can drop below freezing, and pre-action systems are used in buildings where water damage is a concern.
