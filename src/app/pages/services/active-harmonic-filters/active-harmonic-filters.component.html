Active Harmonic Filters

Intro

Active harmonic filters are devices that are used to eliminate harmonic distortion from power systems. Harmonic distortion is caused by nonlinear loads that draw current in a nonlinear manner, causing the voltage and current waveforms to be distorted. This can lead to a range of problems including reduced efficiency, overheating of equipment, and interference with communication systems.

How does it work?

Active harmonic filters work by injecting a current into the power system that is equal in magnitude and opposite in phase to the harmonic current produced by the nonlinear loads. This cancels out the harmonic current and restores the voltage and current waveforms to their sinusoidal shape. The filters are able to detect the harmonic current and produce the appropriate correction signal within a fraction of a cycle, ensuring that the distortion is eliminated in real-time.

Active harmonic filters are typically installed at the point of common coupling (PCC) between the power system and the nonlinear load. They can be used in a range of applications including industrial processes, data centers, hospitals, and commercial buildings. They provide a cost-effective solution for eliminating harmonic distortion and improving the quality and reliability of power systems.
